- Anderson and Nair draft an email regarding Szarfman's data mining activities that Anderson eventually sends to Marks.[^fn-195]
- In the email, Anderson takes issue with Szarfman's efforts and access to CBER data "given that she is a CDER employee."[^fn-196] He notes, "we have concerns about her communicating data mining findings using CBER VAERS data to CBER and non-CBER personnel."[^fn-197] Anderson requests that Szarfman "refrain[s] from using her FDA email or communicating data mining findings using CBER VAERS data[.]"[^fn-198]
- Anderson appears to acknowledge the masking issue Szarfman has previously raised.[^fn-199] Rather than take immediate steps to adjust FDA's system to address Szarfman's concerns, Anderson suggests taking a "retrospective approach" to determine if safety signals were missed only after receiving reports of adverse events through active surveillance systems.[^fn-200] Given that FDA failed to detect the safety signal for myocarditis months earlier, it is unclear what further evidence was needed to trigger a reevaluation of FDA's current data mining system.[^fn-201]
